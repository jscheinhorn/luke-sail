Is it possible to sail on another boat?

It is possible to rotate during sails as long as each boat has enough experienced crew for their skipper to sail. Please check with your skipper(s).

Why a catamaran?

They have two hulls and don't roll as much as a monohull. Comfort and spaciousness.

What's a dinghy?

We ride the dinghy to and from the docks and shore.

Will I have internet?

Most of the time

What safety equipment is there?

Life raft, EPIRB, VHF Radios, life vests, flares. We will have a safety briefing on the first day to go over the safety equipment on the boat and any protocols, including day-to-day practices for safe use of the gas stoves, closing hatches, etc.

Do I need a certification to scuba dive?

Many dive shops will give you a short PADI primer course to train you and will guide you to limited depths (~40ft) if you do not have a certification but still want to dive

Do I need to know how to sail? Will I sail the boat?

The boats are arranged so that there is a sufficient number of trained skippers, experienced crew, and crew on each boat. Skippers have either RYA Day Skipper or ASA Bareboat certification or equivalent experience. There are plenty of sailing duties to help with for willing and able crew, including hoistng and dousing sails, dropping anchor, picking up moorings, being a lookout. These skills can be conferred to you by your skippers/experienced crew. Let your skipper know if you'd like to have a hand on the helm!

What are the basic boat terms I will hear on board?

Pictures and video coming soon!

  • Fore / Forward - anything forward of the mast
  • Aft - anything behind the mast
  • Bow - the very front of the boat
  • Stern - the very back of the boat
  • Port - the left side of the boat if facing the bow
  • Starboard - the right side of the boat if facing the bow
  • Tack - turning the boat so that the origin of the wind comes at us from the bow
  • Gybe / Jibe - turning the boat so that the origin of the wind comes at us from the stern
  • Main sail
  • Jib sail - smaller (generally), forward sail
  • Boom - don't let it hit you!
  • Parts of the sail
    • Tack
    • Clew
    • Head
    • Luff
    • Leach
    • Foot
  • Winch
  • Cabin
  • Cockpit
  • Deck
  • Windward - the side closer to the origin of the wind (used in relation to something downwind)
  • Leeward - the downwind side (used in relation to something that is upwind)
  • Knots - nautical miles per hour, 1.15 miles per hour
